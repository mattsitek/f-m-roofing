Questions for Tom

Understanding your real constraints before we prescribe
Rivet

Before we build a plan that actually fits, we need to understand what's really holding Ferguson & Melvin back. These questions help us figure out if the bottleneck is on the demand side (not enough leads) or the supply side (can't handle more work).

Demand Side

Question 1
Where do your leads come from today? What percentage is referral vs. online vs. builder/GC relationships?
If 90% is referral, the business is healthy but fragile. One bad year from a key referral partner could crater revenue.
Question 2
How many inbound calls or quote requests do you get per week? How many turn into jobs?
This tells us your current close rate and whether more leads would actually translate to more revenue — or just more noise.
Question 3
Do you know how many calls you miss after hours or when the crew is busy?
Most contractors lose 30-40% of calls. If there's no after-hours capture, you're handing leads to competitors every evening and weekend.

Supply Side

Question 4
If we doubled your lead flow tomorrow, could your crew handle it? What's your current backlog?
If you're already booked 6 weeks out, the play isn't more leads — it's pricing power, crew expansion, or filtering for higher-margin jobs.
Question 5
What's your biggest headache right now — finding work, finding workers, or managing the work you have?
This one question tells us everything. It determines whether we focus on marketing, recruiting, or operations.
Question 6
What does a great year look like for Ferguson & Melvin in revenue? What about this year specifically?
We need to know the gap between where you are and where you want to be. It tells us if growth is the goal, or if profitability and margin are the real focus.
